Pork Adobo is the unofficial national dish of the Philippines, and for good reason—it’s the ultimate comfort food. It is a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet stew where pork is simmered until it's incredibly tender. The magic of Adobo isn't just in the taste; it's in the preservation. Historically, the vinegar and salt (from the soy sauce) were used to keep the meat fresh in the tropical heat before refrigeration existed.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Pork Belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/3 cup Vinegar
  • 1 head of Garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp Whole Black Peppercorns
  • 3-5 Dried Bay Leaves
  • 1 cup Water
  • Sugar to taste (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate the pork in soy sauce and garlic for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sear the pork pieces in a hot pot until browned.
  3. Add the marinade liquid, water, peppercorns, and bay leaves; simmer until tender.
  4. Add the vinegar and let it cook for 2 minutes without stirring.
  5. Simmer uncovered until the sauce reaches your desired thickness.
  6. Serve warm with steamed rice.
